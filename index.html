Improving Energy Forecasting in Puerto Rico’s Power Grid

This project highlights the mismatch between energy generation and capacity in Puerto Rico's power grid. By visualizing these mismatches, the project helps identify when and where energy imbalances occur, informing strategies to optimize energy storage, distribution, and production. This can improve grid reliability and support Puerto Rico’s transition to more sustainable energy solutions. Data was sourced from the PR100 Project and cleaned with Excel and Python.

Renewable Electricity Output by Country

This project examines the link between renewable energy use and economic wealth in G20 countries. While it finds a weak but significant connection between renewable electricity and GDP per capita, it shows that other factors also influence renewable energy use. The findings highlight the need for a balanced approach to energy policy and economic development. Data was sourced from Our World in Data and the World Bank and was cleaned using Excel and Python.

Fraud Detection System

This project focuses on detecting and analyzing fraudulent transactions. The dashboard tracks fraud trends, comparing transaction volume, timing, and amounts with legitimate transactions. Dashboards like these serve to help financial institutions identify fraud patterns and improve prevention strategies to reduce financial losses. The data used for this project was artificially generated and processed with SQL.

Customer Churn Analysis

This project analyzes customer churn in the telecom industry by examining factors such as bandwidth usage, monthly charges, and tenure. It identifies patterns that explain why customers leave, offering insights to improve retention strategies. The box plots show that higher usage, lower monthly costs, and longer tenure are associated with lower churn rates. The data for this project came from a fictional telecommunications company as part of a school curriculum and was cleaned and analyzed using Python.
